# Fundamental Analysis Report: Kweichow Moutai (600519.SS)
Report Date: 2025-03-20

## 1. Company Profile
600519.SS (Kweichow Moutai) is China's leading producer of ultra-high-end baijiu (Chinese liquor), with the iconic "Moutai" brand that holds unparalleled pricing power and consumer loyalty in the premium liquor market. The company is a core blue-chip constituent of the A-share market, with a dominant market share in the >RMB 1000 per bottle baijiu segment, supported by decades of brand building and unique production geography (brewed in Maotai town, Guizhou).

## 2. Core Financial Analysis (Latest 4 Reporting Periods)
Based on the latest available financial statements up to 2025 Q3, we summarize key trends:
### Top-line Growth Trend
Revenue growth has decelerated sequentially through 2025:
- 2025 Q1: +10.67% YoY total operating revenue
- 2025 H1 (Q2): +7.26% YoY total operating revenue
- 2025 Q3: +0.35% YoY total operating revenue
- 2024 Full Year: +12.77% YoY total operating revenue

The sharp deceleration in Q3 2025 is primarily driven by ongoing channel restructuring: Moutai is shifting from traditional distributor models to direct-to-consumer (2C) sales, which has led to temporary destocking among existing distributors, suppressing short-term reported revenue. This is a structural adjustment, not a reflection of end-demand weakness.

### Profitability
The company retains its industry-leading profitability metrics, with no signs of margin erosion:
- Gross margin: Consistently 91-92% across all reporting periods (91.29% in 2025 Q3), one of the highest among all A-share listed companies, reflecting unchallenged pricing power for the Moutai brand.
- Net profit margin: Stays above 52% (52.08% in 2025 Q3), showing extremely efficient operating cost control.
- ROE: Cumulative ROE for the first 3 quarters of 2025 is 24.64%, on track for a full-year ROE of ~33%, far above the median ROE of 8-10% for A-share consumer companies.
- Earnings per share (EPS): 51.53 for the first 3 quarters of 2025, compared to 68.64 for full-year 2024, tracking in line with full-year consensus expectations.

### Balance Sheet & Cash Flow
Moutai has one of the healthiest balance sheets in the A-share market:
- Asset-liability ratio: Only 12.8% as of 2025 Q3, down from 19.04% at the end of 2024, meaning the company holds net cash (no material interest-bearing debt), giving it full resilience against economic cycles.
- Book value per share (BPS): 205.28 as of 2025 Q3, up 10.6% from 185.57 at end-2024, reflecting steady net asset accumulation.
- Operating cash flow per share: 30.50 for the first 3 quarters of 2025, with 73.61 for full-year 2024, demonstrating extremely strong cash conversion—Moutai collects payment upfront from distributors, leading to persistent positive operating cash flow.

## 3. Broker Research Consensus
As of the latest March 2026 (recent 1-month) research reports:
- 4 out of 5 brokerages rate the stock a "Buy", 1 rates it "Overweight/Accumulate", showing broad bullish consensus on long-term prospects.
- Consensus 2025 EPS forecast: RMB 71-72.5, implying a current forward P/E of ~20x, which is reasonable for a high-moat consumer staple.
- 2026/2027 consensus EPS forecasts are RMB 72-78 / RMB 77-84, with corresponding forward P/E of 18.6-20.5x / 17.3-20.1x, indicating the market prices in gradual long-term earnings growth from channel reform.
- Key analyst themes: Upside from 2C direct sales transformation, sustained premium demand, and long-term brand value appreciation.

## 4. Key Insights & Trading Implications
1. **Short-term risk**: The Q3 2025 growth deceleration is a temporary headwind from channel restructuring, but it may lead to near-term volatility as the market prices in the slowdown.
2. **Long-term strength**: The core moat (brand, pricing power, unique production advantage) remains fully intact, with no material loss of market share in the ultra-high-end baijiu segment.
3. **Valuation**: The current 20x forward P/E is in line with historical valuation ranges for Moutai, and does not price in excessive optimism, with downside limited by the strong balance sheet and consistent dividend payments (dividend per share is ~RMB 168-169 in recent periods, a ~1.5% dividend yield at current price levels).
4. **Key risk factor: The main downside risk is a deeper-than-expected slowdown in premium consumption in China, but Moutai has historically been more resilient to economic cycles than mid-tier baijiu brands.
