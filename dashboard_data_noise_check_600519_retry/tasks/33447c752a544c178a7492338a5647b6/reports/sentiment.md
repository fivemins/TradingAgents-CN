1. Release of the 2024 full-year annual report on March 17

## 4. Shareholder Structure and Concentration Trend
From the historical trend provided, Moutai has a consistent long-term cyclic pattern: when stock price corrects, retail investors increase holdings (shareholder count rises), and when price rebounds, institutional investors accumulate positions (shareholder count falls). 

As of the most recent 2025-02-28 shareholder count disclosure:
- Total shareholders: 189,000, a **2.1% decrease** from January 2025
- Average household stock market value: increased 3.8% WoW, confirming institutional accumulation

The declining shareholder count indicates that institutional investors are confident in Moutai's medium-term growth, after the ~8% correction in December 2024. This is traditionally a bullish leading signal for Moutai, as institutional positioning usually precedes a 3-6 month sustained rally.

## 5. Key Insights and Implications
### Key Insights
1. **Fundamental sentiment confirms resilient demand**: The strong positive sentiment this week is driven by better-than-expected annual results, which defies market concerns over slowing high-end consumption amid macroeconomic headwinds. Moutai's brand power and pricing power remain intact.
2. **Direct sales expansion is a long-term positive**: The increase in direct online sales of affordable Moutai reduces the company's reliance on third-party dealers, allows Moutai to capture more downstream margin, and helps control channel inventory fluctuations, which improves earnings stability.
3. **Short-term volatility is liquidity-driven, not fundamental**: The minor negative sentiment during the March 14 rotation out of staples was caused by broad market liquidity shifts, not company-specific negative news, so this does not change the core growth thesis.
4. **Institutional positioning is bullish**: The declining shareholder count confirms that institutions have been accumulating Moutai shares after the end-2024 correction, which reduces downside risk in the next 6 months.

### Implications for Market Participants
- **Short-term traders (1-4 week horizon)**: Moutai has rallied 4.2% over the past week, so minor profit taking is possible in the near term. Traders should use any 1-2% dips to enter long positions, as the breakout after the annual report is a strong bullish technical and fundamental signal.
- **Long-term investors (6-12 month horizon)**: Moutai remains the highest-quality consumer staples blue chip in the A-share market, with consistent dividend growth (10%+ annual dividend increase for the past 10 years) and resilient demand. Long-term investors should continue holding positions.
