# Fundamental Analysis Report: Kweichow Moutai (600519.SS)
Report Date: 2025-03-20

## 1. Company Profile
Kweichow Moutai is the iconic leading high-end Baijiu (Chinese liquor) manufacturer in China, holding an unrivaled brand position in the global premium spirits market. With centuries of production heritage and unmatched market dominance in the domestic premium liquor segment, it is a core blue-chip holding for domestic and global institutional investors in the A-share market.

### Key Financial Insights:
1. **Temporary Growth Moderation**: The sequential slowdown in YoY growth across 2025 is driven by two transitory factors: (1) a high base effect from 2024's strong double-digit growth, (2) short-term sales digestion during the company's ongoing channel restructuring (shifting from a distributor-dominated model to direct-to-consumer 2C sales). Brokers widely expect growth to re-accelerate once restructuring is complete.
2. **Unmatched Pricing Power Confirmed**: Gross margin has remained consistently above 91% across all recent reporting periods, with net profit margin holding at ~52% — levels that are industry-leading globally for spirits producers. This confirms Moutai retains its strong brand premium, with no material pricing pressure in the premium liquor market.
3. **Strong Cash Generation and Shareholder Returns**: Operating cash flow per share reached 30.5 CNY in 2025 Q3 YTD, and dividend per share is projected at ~168 CNY for 2025, implying a healthy ~2.5% dividend yield at current valuations. Book value per share grew from 185.57 CNY (end-2024) to 205.28 CNY (Q3 2025), reflecting steady net asset accumulation.

## 3. Institutional Broker Consensus (March 2025)
All 5 major broker research reports released in March 2025 maintain a positive outlook: 4 "Buy" ratings and 1 "Overweight" rating. Key consensus points:
- 2025 consensus EPS forecast ranges from 71.0 CNY to 72.5 CNY, implying a forward P/E ratio of ~20x, which is below Moutai's 5-year historical average P/E of ~25x, indicating the stock is moderately undervalued at current levels.
- Core bullish catalysts: (1) Channel transformation to direct 2C sales will cut intermediate distributor costs and lift long-term margins; (2) Premium Baijiu demand has proven resilient even during broader consumption slowdowns; (3) Long-term growth from product mix upgrading and controlled capacity expansion.
- Forward P/E for 2026 and 2027 is 18.6–20.5x and 17.3–19.5x respectively, reflecting continued earnings growth expectations.

## 4. Key Risks
1. Consumption downside: A slower-than-expected recovery in China's premium consumer spending could pressure near-term sales.
2. Policy risk: New regulations on liquor consumption or advertising could impact near-term sentiment.
3. Execution risk: Channel restructuring may take longer than expected to deliver margin improvements.
