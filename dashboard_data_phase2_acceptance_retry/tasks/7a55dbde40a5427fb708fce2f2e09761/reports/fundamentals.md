# Fundamental Analysis Report: Kweichow Moutai (600519.SS)
Report Date: 2025-03-20 | Industry: High-end Baijiu (Liquor)

## 1. Company Profile
Kweichow Moutai is China's leading and most valuable high-end baijiu producer, with a centuries-old brand reputation and unrivaled pricing power in the global baijiu market. It is a core blue-chip constituent of the A-share market, renowned for consistent profitability, strong cash generation, and a wide economic moat around its brand and production process.

## 2. Core Financial Analysis (Latest Available Data)
The following analysis draws from the 2024 full-year report and 2025 Q1-Q3 (third quarter) report provided in the fundamentals snapshot:
### Key Profitability & Growth Metrics
- **2024 Full Year**: Total operating revenue reached ¥174.14B (+12.77% YoY), net profit attributable to shareholders hit ¥86.23B (+15.38% YoY), with full-year EPS of 68.64, ROE of 36.02%, and gross margin of 91.93%. The company delivered strong double-digit growth in 2024, outperforming many peer baijiu producers.
- **2025 Q1-Q3 (YTD)**: Total operating revenue is ¥130.90B (+6.32% YoY), net profit attributable to shareholders is ¥64.63B (+6.25% YoY), with YTD EPS of 51.53, ROE of 24.64%, and gross margin holding steady at 91.29%.

Growth moderated to mid-single digits in 205 YTD, reflecting a slower recovery in premium consumer spending and a higher base effect from 2024's strong growth, but the company's core profitability remains resilient. Gross margin has stayed above 91% across all reporting periods over the past year, confirming the company's unmatched pricing power and cost control in the high-end baijiu sector.

### Balance Sheet & Cash Flow Health
- Net asset per share (BPS) stood at 205.28 as of 2025 Q3, reflecting strong accumulated net asset growth.
- Cash flow from operations per share is 30.50, indicating robust operating cash generation, consistent with Moutai's dealer prepayment business model.
- The gearing ratio (debt-to-asset) is only 14.7% as of 2025 Q3, with extremely low leverage and no material debt risk. The balance sheet is one of the healthiest among large-cap A-share companies.

## 3. Broker Research Consensus
All 5 recent (March 2026, forward-looking for 2025-2027) broker reports from top Chinese institutions maintain a bullish outlook on Moutai:
- Ratings: 4 "Buy" ratings and 1 "Overweight" rating, with no sell or hold recommendations from the surveyed brokers.
- Consensus earnings forecasts: 2025 EPS is expected to land between 71.07-72.55, implying a forward P/E of ~20.0-20.5x. 2026 EPS is forecast between 71.26-77.89, with a forward P/E of ~18.7-20.5x.
- Key research themes: Brokers widely highlight the company's ongoing marketing reform, channel restructuring (moving to direct 2C sales), and product portfolio optimization as long-term growth drivers that will solidify Moutai's market leading position. Most analysts believe the company's operational inflection point has arrived, with "stronger-for-longer" fundamentals.

## 4. Key Insights for Traders
1. **Core quality remains intact**: Moutai's strong brand moat, consistent high margins, robust cash flow, and healthy balance sheet make it one of the most defensive high-quality large-cap stocks in A-share, suitable for long-term allocation.
2. **Moderating growth is priced in**: The slowdown to mid-single digit growth in 2025 YTD is largely expected by the market, and the current 20x forward P/E is in line with Moutai's historical valuation range, with no material overvaluation risk.
3. **Reforms driving long-term upside**: The ongoing channel and marketing transformations are expected to unlock incremental growth over the next 2-3 years, which is not fully reflected in current earnings forecasts, providing potential upside for patient investors.
