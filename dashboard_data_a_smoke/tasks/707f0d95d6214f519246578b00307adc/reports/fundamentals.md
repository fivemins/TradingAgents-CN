# Fundamental Analysis Report: 300750.SZ (Ningde Era/CATL)
**Report Date: 2025-03-20**

## 1. Company Profile
300750.SZ (Contemporary Amperex Technology Co., Limited, CATL) is the global leading manufacturer of lithium-ion batteries for electric vehicles (EV) and energy storage systems, with leading market share in both domestic and global EV battery segments. The company is focused on R&D, production, and sales of new energy batteries, and is expanding into battery recycling, energy storage services, and new battery technology development.

---

## 2. Core Financial Performance (2025)
Based on the latest 2025 quarterly and full-year financial statements released up to 2025-03-20, CATL showed strong profitability with profit growth significantly outpacing revenue growth, driven by scale effects, cost optimization, and product mix upgrade:
1.  **Top-line Growth**: Full-year 2025 total operating revenue reached 423.7 billion CNY, representing a 17.04% YoY increase. Growth accelerated sequentially through the year: Q1 (+6.18% YoY) → Q2 (+7.27% YoY) → Q3 (+9.28% YoY), with Q4 delivering a sharp acceleration to drive full-year double-digit growth.
2.  **Bottom-line Outperformance**: Full-year attributable net profit reached 72.2 billion CNY, growing 42.28% YoY, more than double the revenue growth rate. Even after deducting non-recurring gains and losses, net profit still grew 43.37% YoY, indicating core operating profitability is very strong.
3.  **Margin Improvement**: Gross sales margin expanded sequentially through 2025, rising from 24.41% in Q1 to 26.27% in the full year, reflecting successful cost controls and a shift towards higher-margin products (such as energy storage batteries and high-energy density EV batteries).
4.  **Per-share Metrics**: Full-year basic EPS reached 16.14 CNY/share, net asset per share (BPS) is 73.87 CNY, and annualized ROE is 24.91%, which is an exceptional level for a large-cap manufacturing company, indicating strong capital efficiency.
5.  **Balance Sheet Health**: The asset-liability ratio decreased slightly from 64.74% in Q1 to 61.94% in full-year 2025, showing gradual deleveraging. Current ratio (1.60) and quick ratio (1.36) are well above safe levels, and operating cash flow per share is 29.19 CNY, indicating strong liquidity and cash generation capacity to support ongoing capacity expansion and R&D investment.

## 3. Broker Consensus and Market Sentiment
All 5 recent broker research reports (released in March 2026 after the 2025 full-year earnings release) hold positive views on CATL:
- 4 out of 5 brokers give a "Buy" rating, 1 gives an "Add" rating, indicating a strong bullish consensus.

- Key bullish catalysts highlighted by analysts:

  2.  Strong profit resilience and margin expansion amid industry competition
  3.  Accelerated capacity expansion to consolidate global market leadership
  4.  Business model evolution from pure product manufacturing to "product + service" (including battery as a service, recycling) driving long-term value growth

## 4. Shareholder Concentration Trend
Historical data shows that since its 2018 IPO, CATL's shareholder count has declined consistently: from 368,718 shareholders in June 2018 to 76,045 shareholders in March 2019, representing a 79.4% drop. This indicates that shareholding has gradually become concentrated in institutional investors, reflecting long-term confidence in the company's growth prospects among professional investors.

---

## 5. Key Insights for Traders
1.  CATL has demonstrated clear operating leverage: as scale expands, profit growth is far outpacing revenue growth, which is a positive signal of improving operating efficiency in a competitive industry.
2.  The sequential acceleration of growth through 2025 indicates that demand is improving rather than weakening, which supports positive earnings momentum into 2026.
3.  The company maintains a strong balance sheet and cash position, enabling it to invest in next-generation battery technologies (solid-state batteries, sodium-ion batteries) to maintain its competitive moat.
4.  Broad consensus among analysts is strongly bullish, with expectations of sustained double-digit earnings growth over the next two years.
