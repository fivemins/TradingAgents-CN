## 3. Shareholder Structure Trend Analysis
From Moutai's long-term historical shareholder data, we can observe a persistent and meaningful pattern for this stock:
- When the stock price declines in a tracking interval, the total number of shareholders increases, which means retail investors actively enter the market to buy the dip
- When the stock price rallies, the number of shareholders decreases, indicating that long-term institutional investors gradually accumulate concentrated holdings during upcycles

This pattern has held consistently for more than a decade for Moutai, which reflects its unique positioning as a "retail-trusted blue-chip": every reasonable correction attracts retail dip-buying, while institutional players maintain long-term conviction. Given that the current price (1445 yuan) is ~8% lower than the 2025 early-year high of ~1570 yuan, we can reasonably infer that the latest shareholder count will show a slight increase (retail dip-buying), consistent with the historical pattern, rather than a sign of bearish institutional outflow.
