Alright, let's cut through the back and forth first. The bull team is leaning hard on Moutai's undisputed brand moat, the fact it's growing twice as fast as the rest of the high-end baijiu sector, 2C channel reform that should lift margins a bit, and recent technical signals pointing to an uptrend, plus they say they've fixed their 2021 mistake of overestimating runaway growth. The bear team pushes back that the bull's growth numbers are misleading, Moutai is already at 45% of the high-end market so it's basically saturated, its valuation is way too expensive for its now-mature growth rate, the 40% rally since last September is already priced around an uncertain future price hike, and rising retail share counts mean institutions are selling into strength rather than buying the dip.

My call here is unequivocally Sell. Let me walk you through why. First, the bear's most unrefuted point is the valuation math that the bull completely failed to counter. The bull says 20x 2025 forward P/E is at the low end of its 5-year range, but that range was set when Moutai was growing 12-15% a year, not the 6% or less we're seeing now. Even if you take the bull's own generous 8% projected CAGR, the PEG ratio is 2.5, way above the 1-1.5 benchmark for mature consumer staples. If you use the far more realistic full-year 2025 <1% growth figure the bear laid out (since the YTD YoY number is skewed by a weak 2024 base), that PEG is even higher. There's no way around it: Moutai is significantly overvalued for its current growth trajectory.

Second, the bull's growth claims don't hold up under scrutiny. That 6.32% YTD YoY growth they're touting only looks good because the first half of 2024 was weak, the sequential run rate puts full year 2025 growth at less than 1% over 2024. That's not a temporary base effect, that's a material slowdown driven by soft consumer spending on premium gifting and business events, which makes that widely hyped price hike far less of a sure thing. Even if the price hike does happen, it's already fully priced into the rally we've seen over the last 8 months, so there's almost no upside left if it hits, and 15-20% downside if it's delayed or smaller than expected. The risk-reward is clearly lopsided to the downside.

Third, the bull completely misrepresented the shareholder pattern data. They said retail dip buying is normal, but the rising share count after the recent 8% pullback means institutions are offloading shares to retail investors, not accumulating. Most major domestic institutions are already at max allocation to Moutai, so there are no new marginal buyers left to push prices higher once the price hike hype fades. That golden cross the bull is leaning on is a lagging indicator that just reflects the past rally, not future upside, and the recent near-overbought RSI points to overextension, not a healthy uptrend.

I'm not making the old 2018/2021 bear mistake of writing off Moutai's core moat entirely, it's still a fantastic company, but great companies don't make great investments at inflated valuations, and we're also avoiding the 2021 bull mistake of buying into hype over cold valuation math.
