## 2. Rationale
We reject both full Sell and full Buy positions to avoid repeating prior costly mistakes: the 2021 bull error of buying into hype over valuation math, and the 2018/2021 bear error of writing off Moutai’s irreplicable brand moat to sell prematurely.
- **Full Sell is unwarranted**: Moutai’s status as a core holding for all Chinese institutional and pension capital, plus its 10+ year track record of retail dip-buying, support a persistent valuation premium that makes the bear’s 12x P/E fair value estimate unrealistic. The 8% recent pullback stabilized with no panic selling, confirming downside is capped at ~10%, far lower than the 15-20% downside the original sell case claimed. Selling 75% of holdings would forfeit 60%+ of potential long-term upside from ongoing 2C channel reform, an unacceptable opportunity cost for one of A-shares’ most consistent cash generators.

Near-term price hike expectations and 1-2 years of 2C margin gains are already fully priced into the 40% 8-month rally, and institutional profit-taking means there are limited large marginal buyers to drive near-term upside. Adding new positions here exposes investors to unnecessary drawdown risk if the expected price hike is delayed or smaller than consensus.
- **Hold is strongly justified**: The risk-reward profile is evenly balanced, with ~10% downside capped by retail support and ~15-20% long-term upside from full 2C reform rollout and sustained institutional core allocations. Holding the majority of the position with partial profit taking eliminates the worst risks of both extreme positions, aligning with both capital protection and growth mandates.
