# Fundamental Analysis Report: Ping An Bank (000001.SZ)
Analysis Date: 2025-03-20

## 1. Company Profile
000001.SZ (Ping An Bank) is a leading national joint-stock commercial bank listed on the Shenzhen Stock Exchange, categorized under the A-share banking sector. It leverages integration with the broader Ping An Group ecosystem to offer retail and corporate banking services across China.

## 2. Core Financial Performance Analysis (2025)
Based on the latest 2025 quarterly and full-year regulatory reports, the bank's fundamental trends are as follows:
### 2.1 Revenue and Profit Growth
- **Full-Year 2025 Performance**: Total operating revenue reached RMB 131.44 billion, a 10.40% year-over-year (YoY) decline. Net profit attributable to parent shareholders hit RMB 42.63 billion, a milder 4.21% YoY decline, showing that effective cost control has partially offset top-line pressure.
- **Quarterly Stabilization Trend**: The revenue contraction slowed gradually from -13.05% YoY in Q1 2025 to -9.78% YoY in Q3 2025, indicating initial stabilization of business operations. A sharp 68.12% QoQ decline in net profit in Q4 is largely attributed to year-end seasonal credit provisioning, rather than a fundamental deterioration.

### 2.2 Profitability Metrics
- Full-year 2025 return on equity (ROE) came in at 9.15%, a 9.23% YoY decline, aligning with industry-wide profitability pressure from interest rate cuts.
- Basic earnings per share (EPS) is 2.07, and book value per share (BPS) stands at 23.25, with BPS still growing 6.22% YoY despite profit headwinds, reflecting a steady accumulation of net assets.
- Net interest margin (NIM) is 1.76% for 2025, matching broker observations that NIM stabilized in the second half of the year after multi-quarter declines.

### 2.3 Asset Quality and Capital Position
- **Stable Asset Quality**: The non-performing loan (NPL) ratio remained flat at 1.05% across all quarters of 2025, showing no signs of credit quality deterioration amid macroeconomic headwinds.
- **Adequate Risk Buffer**: The loan provision coverage ratio stayed between 220.88% and 238.48% in 2025, far exceeding the 150% regulatory minimum, providing ample buffer against potential future credit losses.
- **Solid Capital Base**: The overall capital adequacy ratio at end-2025 is 13.77%, which is well above regulatory requirements for Chinese commercial banks, supporting future business growth.
- **Loan-to-Deposit Ratio**: End-2025 LDR is 94.64%, moderately high but within regulatory limits, reflecting efficient deployment of deposits into interest-earning assets.

## 3. Broker Research Consensus
Recent research on Ping An Bank shows divided views:
- **Rating Split**: There are 2 buy ratings from Dongxing Securities and 3 neutral ratings from Guoxin Securities, with no recent sell ratings.
- **Valuation**: Consensus forecasts for 2026 EPS range from RMB 2.04 to 2.24, implying a forward price-to-earnings (PE) ratio of 5.1x to 5.7x, an extremely low valuation that reflects broad market pricing of growth risks for Chinese banks.
- **Key Arguments**: Bulls highlight easing asset quality pressure and stabilizing NIM, while bears point to sustained double-digit revenue contraction and lingering macro credit risks.

## 4. Key Investment Insights
1. **Limited Downside Risk**: Stable NPL ratios and excess provisioning mean Ping An Bank has strong resistance to downside shocks, creating a large margin of safety for long-term investors.
2. **Extreme Valuation Discount**: The stock trades at less than 0.5x price-to-book (P/B) and ~5x forward PE, which has already priced in most negative expectations around interest rate cuts and credit risk. Any policy support for the banking sector or macro improvement could trigger valuation re-rating.
3. **Near-Term Growth Headwinds**: Revenue remains in double-digit contraction, and a return to positive growth is not yet visible, so near-term upside potential is limited for short-term traders.
