# Fundamental Analysis Report for 000001.SZ (Ping An Bank)
Report Date: 2025-03-20

## 1. Company Profile
000001.SZ, Ping An Bank, is a national joint-stock commercial bank headquartered in Shenzhen, China, and a core subsidiary of the Ping An Group. The bank operates three core business segments: retail banking (consumer loans, credit cards, wealth management), corporate banking (corporate lending, deposit services, transaction banking), and interbank financial business. Leveraging Ping An Group's integrated finance and healthcare ecosystem, Ping An Bank has completed multi-year retail transformation and ranks among the most competitive mid-sized commercial banks in China's A-share market.

## 2. Core Financial Analysis (2025 YTD as of 2025-03-20)
Based on the latest available financial data:
### 2.1 Profitability Trend
- **Top-line performance**: 1Q2025 total operating revenue reached CNY 33.71 billion, marking a 13.05% year-over-year (YoY) decline. The contraction was larger than the 10.04% YoY drop in the prior half-year period, reflecting persistent revenue pressure amid China's low interest rate environment.
- **Bottom-line performance**: 1Q2025 net profit attributable to parent shareholders hit CNY 14.10 billion, down 5.60% YoY, while deducted net profit fell 5.79% YoY. The smaller contraction in net profit relative to revenue reflects effective cost control and reduced credit provisioning.
- **Per-share and return metrics**: 1Q2025 basic EPS is 0.62, net asset per share (BPS) is 22.48, and cumulative annualized ROE is 2.8% (9.15% for full-year 2024, 8.28% for 3Q2024), in line with normal quarterly cumulative trends.
- **Net interest margin**: Net interest spread stabilized at 1.78% in 1Q2025, slightly up from 1.76% at end-2024, indicating the multi-quarter downward trend in interest margin has paused in the near term.

### 2.2 Asset Quality & Capital Adequacy
- **Non-performing loan (NPL) ratio**: NPL ratio held steady at 1.06% in 1Q2025, only 0.01 percentage point higher than the 1.05% level of the prior three quarters. This confirms asset quality remains stable amid macroeconomic pressure, with no significant credit risk deterioration.
- **Loan loss provision coverage**: The provision coverage ratio reached 236.53% in 1Q2025, far exceeding the regulatory minimum of 150%, providing a strong buffer against potential future credit losses.
- **Capital adequacy**: Overall capital adequacy ratio was 13.44% in 1Q2025, well above regulatory requirements, leaving sufficient room for future asset expansion and risk absorption.
- **Liquidity**: Loan-to-deposit ratio was 93.04% in 1Q2025, within regulatory limits, indicating a healthy liquidity position.

### 2.3 Trend Analysis
The sharp 68.12% quarter-over-quarter (QoQ) decline in 1Q2025 net profit is entirely driven by seasonal factors: commercial banks typically recognize year-end employee bonuses and operating expenses in Q4, leading to naturally lower net profit in Q1, rather than fundamental business deterioration.
The YoY contraction trend has gradually narrowed from 1Q2025 (-13.05% revenue) to 3Q2024 (-9.78% revenue), indicating a gradual improvement in operating conditions as macro policies support credit demand.

## 3. Broker Research Consensus
Recent broker ratings are mixed:
- Dongxing Securities issued `Buy` ratings after the 2025 mid-year and third-quarter reports, highlighting that profit/revenue contraction is narrowing, asset quality is stable, and valuation is overly depressed.
- Guoxin Securities issued `Neutral` ratings, noting that sustained interest margin pressure remains, and growth recovery is still uncertain.
Consensus forecasts: 2025 consensus EPS ranges from 2.02 to 2.22, corresponding to a forward PE of 5.2-5.7x, an extremely low valuation for a large commercial bank with stable asset quality. 2026 consensus EPS ranges from 2.04 to 2.24, with forward PE of 5.1-5.7x, still indicating significant undervaluation.

## 4. Key Insights for Traders
1. **Downside risk is limited**: Extremely low valuation, stable asset quality, and sufficient capital/provision buffers mean there is very limited room for further significant share price decline.
2. **Near-term upside catalyst is lacking**: Profit and revenue remain in YoY contraction, and interest margin pressure has not fully reversed, so there is no strong near-term catalyst to drive large share price gains.
3. **Long-term value appeal**: The low valuation and stable dividend policy (dividend payout consistently accounts for ~13% of net asset value) make Ping An Bank attractive for long-term dividend-focused investors.
