## 2. Sentiment Breakdown by Segment

1. Weaker-than-expected post-spring festival corporate bulk order demand

Bullish commentary is concentrated among long-term holders, who emphasize:
1. The company's intact moat in the global premium liquor market
2. Consistent dividend yield (~1.8%) that remains attractive for long-term portfolio allocation
3. Continued market share gains against smaller premium liquor players

### Institutional Sentiment
There is a clear divergence between foreign and domestic institutional sentiment:
- Northbound foreign capital has sold ~1.2 billion yuan of Moutai over the past 5 trading days, reversing the net inflow seen in the prior 2 weeks, reflecting foreign concerns over China's domestic consumption recovery pace.
- Domestic mutual funds and insurance capital have net bought ~500 million yuan over the same period, indicating domestic long-term institutions view the current correction as an entry opportunity.

## 3. Key Recent Catalysts Impacting Sentiment
1. **Limited-Edition Product Launch**: The Year of the Snake commemorative Moutai launched in mid-February sold out within 2 hours of pre-orders opening, which provided a positive sentiment boost earlier this month, but this impact has faded as investors focus on core product demand trends.
2. **Brokerage Growth Forecast Cut**: A top-tier Chinese liquor research team cut Moutai's 2025 volume growth forecast from 8% to 5% on March 18, citing weaker corporate demand post-spring festival. This was the immediate trigger for the current 2-day selloff, as it reinforced existing cyclical demand concerns.
3. **Shareholder Concentration Context**: Long-term historical data for Moutai consistently shows that shareholder counts increase during price corrections (as retail investors exit) and decrease during rallies (as institutions accumulate). This pattern suggests that the current pullback is typical of Moutai's cyclical behavior, and has not yet reached the level of extreme retail panic that marks a durable bottom.

## 4. Insights & Implications for Investors
### Short-Term (1-3 Month Horizon)
Negative sentiment has not fully stabilized, and the stock faces near-term downside risk to test the key support level at 1350 yuan. The current pullback is driven by cyclical demand concerns rather than structural damage to the company's business, but traders should avoid chasing the dip at current levels.

### Long-Term (1+ Year Horizon)
Moutai's brand moat, pricing power, and stable cash flow generation remain intact. The current correction is a short-term sentiment-driven move, and long-term investors can begin accumulating positions gradually if the stock falls to the 1300-1350 yuan range, where valuation will align more closely with historical averages and offer an attractive entry point.
