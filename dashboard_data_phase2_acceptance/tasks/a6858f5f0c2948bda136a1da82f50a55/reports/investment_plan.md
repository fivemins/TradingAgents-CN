Unpriced core demand weakness: The 2025 volume cut was a negative surprise, not an expected base effect, as post-Spring Festival bulk demand (Moutai's highest-margin, largest revenue segment) missed even muted consensus forecasts. 2C reform margin upside is unproven, with limited data to confirm it will drive earnings beats.

### Key Caveats That Weaken The Thesis
1. Valuation data discrepancy: The bull case uses low-end broker EPS estimates while the bear case uses market consensus, creating a wide 1350-1550 fair value range that leaves room for 1-2 months of extended rangebound volatility.
2. 2C reform uncertainty: There is no hard, multi-quarter data confirming the channel shift will deliver projected margin uplift or demand stabilization; all upside from the reform is currently forecasted, not realized.
3. Macro consumption tail risk: A slower-than-expected Chinese consumer recovery would disproportionately hit Moutai's core gifting/banquet demand segment, leading to further downward earnings revisions.
4. Sentiment neutrality: Current 48% bearish sentiment is a neutral signal, not a contrarian buy or sell, so no clear directional trend is guaranteed in the near term.
