# Fundamental Analysis Report for Kweichow Moutai (600519.SS)
Analysis Date: 2025-03-20

---

## 1. Company Profile
Kweichow Moutai (600519.SS) is the world's largest liquor company by market capitalization, specializing in premium soy-sauce aroma baijiu (Chinese distilled liquor). It holds an irreplaceable brand moat in China's high-end gifting and business consumption scenarios, with decades of reputation for product quality and inherent product scarcity.

---

## 2. Core Financial Performance (Latest Periods)
Based on 2025 Q3 (latest available report), 2025 half-year, 2025 Q1, and 2024 full-year financial disclosures:
### Top-line & Bottom-line Growth
- For the first three quarters of 2025, total operating revenue reached **130.90 billion CNY**, a 6.32% year-over-year (YoY) increase, consistent with market expectations for mature large-cap growth.
- Net profit attributable to parent shareholders hit **64.63 billion CNY**, growing 6.25% YoY. The small gap between revenue and profit growth is driven by minor short-term cost increases, with no structural impact on profitability.
- Full year 2024 performance showed stronger acceleration: revenue grew 12.77% YoY to 174.14 billion CNY, while net profit grew 16.21% YoY to 86.23 billion CNY, demonstrating the company's ability to deliver upside when market conditions improve.
- Earnings per share (EPS) for 9M 2025 is **51.53 CNY**, up 6.42% YoY, maintaining steady per-share value creation.

### Profitability & Margins
- Gross margin has remained consistently above 91% across all recent periods (91.29% in 9M 2025, 91.30% in H1 2025, 91.93% in 2024 full-year) — this is one of the highest gross margins among global consumer goods companies, reflecting Moutai's unmatched pricing power and strong brand premium, where consumers willingly pay a large premium for its products.
- Net profit margin is consistently above 50% (52.08% in 9M 2025), demonstrating extremely efficient operations: raw material costs account for a tiny share of final product pricing, with most value derived from brand intangibles.
- Annualized return on equity (ROE) for 9M 2025 is 24.64%, far exceeding the consumer staples sector average, indicating strong capital efficiency and consistent value creation for shareholders.

### Balance Sheet & Liquidity
- Book value per share (BPS) as of Q3 2025 is 205.28 CNY, growing 8.48% YoY, with steady organic book value expansion.
- Asset-liability ratio is only 12.81% as of Q3 2025, an extremely low level for a large-cap company. Moutai holds massive net cash on its balance sheet with almost no interest-bearing debt, eliminating any material solvency risk.
- The current ratio is 12.81, indicating extremely strong short-term liquidity, easily covering all near-term operating obligations.

### Cash Flow Generation
- Operating cash flow per share for 9M 2025 is 30.50 CNY. Moutai's business model requires upfront payment from distributors, leading to consistent strong cash conversion that underpins stable dividends and shareholder returns.

## 3. Broker Research Consensus
As of March 2025 (near the analysis date), 5 recent broker reports show overwhelmingly positive sentiment: 4 **Buy** ratings and 1 **Overweight (Add)** rating. Key consensus highlights:
1.  **Channel Reform Catalyst**: Most reports focus on Moutai's ongoing transformation from a distributor-dominated model to direct-to-consumer (2C) sales. This restructuring is expected to cut intermediate profit layers, capture more margin for the company, and improve control over product pricing and inventory.
2.  **Earnings & Valuation**: Consensus 2025 EPS forecasts range from 71.07 to 72.55 CNY, corresponding to a forward P/E of ~20x at current price levels, which is viewed as a reasonable valuation for a high-moat mature growth company. 2026 consensus EPS ranges from 71.26 to 77.85 CNY (forward P/E 18.6-20.5x), and 2027 consensus EPS ranges from 72.78 to 84.02 CNY (forward P/E 17.3-20.1x), pricing in consistent low double-digit to mid-single-digit long-term growth.
3.  **Cycle Resilience: Brokers emphasize that Moutai's premium brand positioning makes it highly resilient to economic downturns, with stable demand from high-end consumption that is less sensitive to macro slowdowns.

## 4. Key Insights for Traders
### Strengths
- Unmatched global brand moat in the premium liquor segment
- Extremely stable high margins, pristine low-debt balance sheet, and consistent strong cash generation
- Ongoing channel reform that can unlock incremental margin upside over the medium term
- Solid dividend payout track record, attractive for long-term income and growth investors
- Core holding for Chinese institutional investors, providing strong price stability

### Risks
- Growth has moderated to mid-single digits in the latest 9M period, which may disappoint investors expecting high double-digit growth
- Valuation is not cheap at ~20x forward P/E (though in-line with Moutai's historical average valuation)
- Minor long-term regulatory risks related to liquor taxation and consumption policies
