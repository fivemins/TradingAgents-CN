# Fundamental Analysis Report: Ping An Bank (000001.SZ)
Analysis Date: 2025-03-20

## 1. Company Profile
000001.SZ, Ping An Bank, is a national joint-stock commercial bank incorporated in China, affiliated with the Ping An Group. The bank focuses on retail banking, corporate banking, and interbank business, leveraging the group's integrated financial ecosystem to drive growth. As of 2025, it maintains a total balance sheet size exceeding 5.3 trillion CNY, with core business focused on loans and deposits.

## 2. Core Financial Analysis (2025 Financial Reports)
### Profitability Trend
- **Earnings per Share (EPS):** Cumulative EPS for 2025 full year is 2.07 CNY, up from 0.62 CNY in Q1 2025. Sequentially, Q4 2025 EPS is ~0.20 CNY, lower than the average 0.62 CNY per quarter in the first three quarters, indicating Q4 profit pressure.
- **Revenue & Profit Growth:** 2025 total operating revenue reached 131.44 billion CNY, **down 10.4% year-over-year (YoY)**. Net profit attributable to parent shareholders was 42.63 billion CNY, **down only 4.21% YoY**. The smaller decline in net profit compared to revenue reflects effective cost control and expense management by the bank, partially offsetting top-line pressure from narrowing net interest spreads.
- **Return on Equity (ROE):** Full year 2025 ROE is 9.15%, a decrease of ~4.9 percentage points YoY, aligning with the overall profit slowdown in the banking sector this cycle.

### Balance Sheet & Asset Quality
- **Capital Position:** Core capital adequacy ratio improved to 13.77% by the end of 2025, up from 13.26% at mid-year, meeting regulatory requirements comfortably, and providing sufficient buffer for future lending expansion.
- **Loan & Deposit Scale:** Total deposits reached 3.58 trillion CNY, total gross loans 3.39 trillion CNY. Loan-to-deposit ratio (LTDR) is 94.64%, slightly down from 96.14% in Q3 2025, maintaining stable liquidity.
- **Asset Quality:** The non-performing loan (NPL) ratio has remained **stable at 1.05% across all quarters in 2025**, showing no sign of rising credit risk. Loan loss provision coverage ratio is 220.88%, which is still far above the regulatory minimum requirement, meaning the bank has fully provisioned for potential bad debt, with limited downside risk from asset quality.
- **Asset-Liability Ratio:** Leverage (asset-liability ratio) is 90.7% at end-2025, slightly down from 91.3% at the start of the year, showing slight deleveraging trend.

## 3. Broker Research Consensus
Recent broker research shows mixed views:
- **Buy Ratings (Dongxing Securities):** Analysts highlight that net interest spread has staged a sequential stabilization since Q3 2025, asset quality remains overall stable, and retail asset quality has improved in the first half of the year. They forecast 2025 EPS of 2.22 CNY, with forward P/E around 5.2x.
- **Neutral Ratings (Guosen Securities):** While acknowledging that asset quality pressure has reduced after full year 2025 results, analysts remain cautious about the sustained pressure on revenue growth amid the low interest rate environment. The latest 2026 consensus EPS forecast is 2.08-2.24 CNY, corresponding to a forward P/E of 5.1-5.2x, indicating an extremely cheap valuation that already prices in most negative growth expectations.

## 4. Key Insights for Traders
1. **Growth Pressure is Real, but Risk is Contained:** The bank is facing significant top-line pressure from slowing revenue (down 10% YoY), but net profit decline is much smaller, and asset quality is fully stable with adequate provisioning. There is low probability of negative surprise from credit risk.
2. **Extremely Low Valuation Offers Downside Protection:** With forward P/E below 5.5x and price-to-book ratio below 0.7x (implied by BPS 23.25 CNY), the valuation is already at historically low levels, which has largely reflected the market's pessimistic expectations for banking sector growth.
3. **Improving Capital Position Supports Future Dividends and Growth:** The rising capital adequacy ratio means the bank has capacity to maintain stable dividend payouts and support future loan growth, which is a positive for long-term shareholders.
