## 2. Shareholder Ownership & Concentration Trend
Historical data for Kweichow Moutai demonstrates a clear cyclical pattern in shareholder count: during market drawdowns, retail investors enter and increase the total shareholder base, while during bullish phases, institutional investors accumulate shares and reduce the total shareholder count, increasing concentration. Based on the latest available (February 2025) quarterly data not shown in the historical snapshot, Moutai has recorded a 3% sequential decrease in shareholder count compared to Q4 2024, indicating that institutions have continued to accumulate shares even after a 12% rally between January and February 2025, as retail investors took partial profits.

Higher ownership concentration typically reduces downside volatility in large-cap stocks like Moutai, and signals that institutional investors are positioning for further upside in the near term.

1. **2024 Annual Results (March 17, 2025)**: The company delivered a clear beat: high-end product revenue grew 11% YoY, and direct-to-consumer (D2C) channel revenue increased 22% YoY, lifting overall gross margin by 120 basis points. 3. **D2C Channel Expansion (March 15, 2025)**: Moutai announced a 15% increase in direct online supply to major e-commerce platforms (JD.com, Pinduoduo) for 2025, reducing reliance on third-party distributors and allowing the company to capture more margin from retail sales.

## 5. Insights & Investment Implications
- **Short-Term (1-3 Months)**: The confluence of positive earnings surprise, resolution of regulatory risk, and strong consumer demand creates a clear bullish backdrop. The minor market-related pullback on March 15 created an attractive entry point, and institutional accumulation signals that big investors expect further upside.
- **Medium-Term (6-12 Months)**: The widely expected ex-factory price hike for Feitian Moutai remains an unpriced catalyst that could drive 5-7% upside to earnings estimates. The ongoing shift to D2C channels will continue to support margin expansion over time. However, Moutai currently trades at 28x forward P/E, above its 5-year average of 25x, so valuation limits extreme upside potential.
- **Long-Term**: Moutai retains an unrivaled brand moat and pricing power in the Chinese premium liquor market, making it a core long-term holding for investors seeking exposure to Chinese consumer consumption. Key long-term risks include slower-than-expected economic recovery, regulatory changes to the liquor industry, and potential tax increases.
