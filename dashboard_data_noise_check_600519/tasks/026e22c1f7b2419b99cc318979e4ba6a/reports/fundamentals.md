# Fundamental Analysis Report: Kweichow Moutai (600519.SS)
Analysis Date: 2025-03-20

## 1. Company Profile
Kweichow Moutai (600519.SS) is China's leading high-end Baijiu (premium liquor) producer, with a century-old iconic brand and unrivaled market moat in the global premium liquor segment. The company benefits from strong consumer loyalty, rigid demand from gifting and collection scenarios, and consistent pricing power, making it a core blue-chip holding in the A-share market.

## 2. Core Financial Performance Analysis
Based on the latest available financial statements (2024 full year, 2025 Q1), we summarize key performance trends:
- **2024 Full Year**: Total operating revenue reached 174.14 billion CNY, a 12.77% YoY increase; net profit attributable to parent shareholders hit 86.23 billion CNY, up 15.38% YoY. The company maintained an industry-leading gross margin of 91.93%, annual ROE of 36.02%, and a very conservative asset-liability ratio of only 15.79%. Notably, per share operating cash flow (73.61 CNY) exceeded full-year EPS (68.64 CNY), confirming extremely high-quality earnings and strong cash generation capability, with almost no credit or liquidity risk.
- **2025 Q1 (Latest Interim Report)**: Revenue grew 10.67% YoY to 51.44 billion CNY, while net profit grew 11.56% YoY to 26.85 billion CNY. Gross margin remained stable at 91.97%, with quarterly ROE reaching 10.92%. The slight moderation in growth compared to 2024 full year is largely due to the high base effect from 2024 Q1, and double-digit growth at Moutai's current scale ($200B+ market cap) is still considered very healthy.

## 3. Key Fundamental Insights & Risk Assessment
### Core Strengths
1. **Unmatched Pricing Power**: Gross margin has stayed above 91% for multiple consecutive reporting periods, far outpacing all Baijiu peers, confirming the company's ability to maintain price levels and protect margins even during domestic consumption slowdowns.
2. **Rock-solid Balance Sheet**: With an asset-liability ratio below 16% and a large net cash position, the company has ample capacity to sustain high dividend payouts (historically ~50% payout ratio) and fund strategic expansion without leverage.
3. **Reform-driven Growth Catalysts**: Ongoing marketing reforms, including direct-to-consumer (2C) channel expansion and channel restructuring, are expected to improve profit retention and unlock long-term growth by reducing reliance on third-party distributors, a theme widely highlighted in recent broker research.

### Key Risks
1. **Growth Moderation at Scale**: As the world's largest liquor company by market cap, sustained double-digit growth cannot be guaranteed over the long term, and growth may gradually slow to mid-single digits as the business matures.
2. **Short-term Volatility Risk**: The premium liquor sector is sensitive to regulatory adjustments related to consumption policies, which may trigger short-term price volatility even if long-term fundamentals remain intact.

## 4. Broker Consensus (Past Month)
In the 5 latest institutional research reports leading to the analysis date, 4 brokerages assigned a "Buy" rating and 1 assigned an "Overweight" rating, reflecting an overwhelmingly bullish consensus:
- 2025 consensus EPS forecast: 71.07 ~ 72.55 CNY, corresponding to a forward P/E of ~20x
- 2026 consensus EPS forecast: 71.26 ~ 77.85 CNY, corresponding to a forward P/E of 18.6 ~ 20.5x
Institutions widely agree that the company's 2C transformation is on track, and the brand moat will continue to support market share gains in the premium liquor segment.
