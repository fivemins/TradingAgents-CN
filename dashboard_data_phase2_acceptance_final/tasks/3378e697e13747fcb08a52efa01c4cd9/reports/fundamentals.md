# Fundamental Analysis Report: 300750.SZ (Ningde Times CATL)
*Analysis Date: 2025-03-20*

## 1. Company Overview
300750.SZ (Ningde Times New Energy Technology, CATL) is the global leading manufacturer of power lithium-ion batteries for new energy vehicles (EVs) and energy storage systems, headquartered in Fujian, China. It is the core supplier for most global and domestic EV automakers, holding the largest global market share in power batteries for consecutive years.

## 2. Core Financial Performance Analysis (2025 Full Year & Interim Reports)
Based on the latest 2025 financial disclosures, the company shows strong top-line and bottom-line growth, with profit expansion outpacing revenue growth:
- **Scale & Growth**: 2025 full-year total operating revenue reached 423.70 billion CNY, representing a 17.04% year-over-year (YoY) increase. Net profit attributable to parent shareholders hit 72.20 billion CNY, jumping **42.28% YoY**, and deducted non-recurring profit grew 43.37% YoY to 64.51 billion CNY. This indicates that the company's profitability improvement is driven by core business rather than non-recurring gains, reflecting strong operational efficiency.
- **Per Share Metrics**: Full-year basic EPS is 16.14 CNY, up 39.38% YoY, and net asset per share (BPS) is 73.87 CNY, up 31.72% YoY, showing continuous shareholder value creation.
- **Profitability**: Return on Equity (ROE) reached 24.91% in 2025, an extremely strong level for a large-cap company, indicating high capital utilization efficiency. Gross margin stabilized at 26.27%, and net profit margin maintained at ~17%, showing that the company's scale effect and technological leadership have effectively offset upstream raw material price volatility.
- **Solvency & Liquidity**: Asset-liability ratio is 61.94%, which is moderate for the manufacturing sector. Current ratio is 1.60 and quick ratio is 1.36, both above the warning line, indicating sufficient short-term liquidity. Operating cash flow per share is 29.19 CNY, far exceeding EPS, showing that the company has very strong cash collection ability and healthy cash conversion cycle.
- **Receivables Management**: Accounts receivable turnover is 59.70%, indicating that the company has strong bargaining power in the industrial chain and maintains good collection efficiency from downstream customers.

Quarterly trend shows that growth has accelerated sequentially: YoY revenue growth was 6.18% in Q1 2025, 7.27% in H1, 9.28% in Q3, and accelerated to 17.04% for full-year, showing that downstream demand (EV + energy storage) picked up gradually in 2025, and the company's capacity expansion began to release benefits.

## 3. Institutional Broker Consensus
In the latest round of research reports (March 2026, reflecting latest outlook as of 2025 full-year report disclosure):
- All 5 tracked research reports maintain positive ratings: 4 "Buy" and 1 "Outperform/Increase Position".
- Consensus earnings forecast: 2026 expected EPS is ~20.3, corresponding to forward PE of ~18-19x, 2027 expected EPS ~24.7, forward PE ~15-16x. This indicates that the market expects the company to maintain 20-25% annual EPS growth in the next two years, and the current valuation is reasonably priced for a industry leader with high growth.
- Key consensus views: 1) The company's technological iteration (sodium battery, condensed battery) maintains lead in the industry; 2) Capacity expansion supports continuous growth in market share; 3) The energy storage business is becoming a new growth driver, driving the company's growth model from product sales to "product + service" evolution.

## 4. Shareholder Concentration Trend
Historically, the company's shareholder count has continued to decline since IPO: from 368,718 in June 2018 to less than 100,000 in late 2018, showing continuous increasing shareholding concentration, which reflects that institutional investors continue to increase their positions and are optimistic about the company's long-term value.

## 5. Key Investment Insights
### Positive Factors:
1.  Profit growth significantly outpaces revenue growth, driven by scale effect and product structure upgrade, showing strong profit elasticity.
2.  As the global industry leader, it benefits from long-term growth of EV penetration and energy storage demand, with clear growth space for the next 3-5 years.
3.  Very high ROE and strong operating cash flow provide support for continuous capacity expansion and R&D investment.
4.  Institutional investors are generally optimistic, with consistent positive outlook.

### Potential Risks:
1.  Intensified industry competition may put pressure on gross margin in the next 1-2 years.
2.  Valuation is not cheap at 19x 2026 forecast PE, so short-term volatility may exist if demand is lower than expected.
