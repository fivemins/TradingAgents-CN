# Fundamental Analysis Report for 000001.SZ (Ping An Bank)
Report Date: 2025-03-20

## 1. Company Profile
000001.SZ is Ping An Bank, a national joint-stock commercial bank listed on the Shenzhen Stock Exchange, and a core financial subsidiary of the Ping An Group. The bank focuses on retail banking, corporate banking, and inter-bank business, with a particular advantage in retail credit and inclusive finance business connected to the Ping An ecosystem.

## 2. Financial Performance Trend (2025)
Based on the latest 2025 quarterly/annual reports, Ping An Bank is facing continued pressure on revenue and profit, with marginal improvement in downward trends:
- **Overall Scale**: By the end of 2025, total deposits reached ~3.58 trillion CNY, total gross loans reached ~3.39 trillion CNY, maintaining steady asset scale expansion.
- **Revenue & Profit**: All 2025 reporting periods show year-over-year (YoY) declines, but the decline rate is gradually narrowing:
  - 2025 Q1: Total operating revenue down 13.05% YoY, net profit attributable to parent (NP) down 5.60% YoY
  - 2025 Mid-Year: Total operating revenue down 10.04% YoY, NP down 3.90% YoY
  - 2025 Q3: Total operating revenue down 9.78% YoY, NP down 3.50% YoY
  - 2025 Full Year: Total operating revenue ~131.44 billion CNY (-10.40% YoY), NP ~42.63 billion CNY (-4.21% YoY)
- **Per Share Indicators**: 2025 full-year EPS is 2.07 CNY, net assets per share (BPS) is 23.25 CNY, weighted ROE is 9.15%, all indicators declined slightly compared to the previous year, in line with the overall profit trend.
- **Interest Spread**: Net interest spread stabilized at ~1.76%-1.78% across all 2025 quarters, which matches broker commentary that interest spread has reached a stage bottom.

## 3. Asset Quality & Capital Adequacy
Ping An Bank maintains stable asset quality and sufficient risk resistance capacity:
- **Non-Performing Loan (NPL) Ratio**: The NPL ratio remained stable at 1.05% throughout all 2025 reporting periods, which is lower than the average level of domestic joint-stock banks, indicating controlled credit risk.
- **Provision Coverage**: Loan loss provision coverage ratio stayed above 220% (220.88% at end-2025), far exceeding the regulatory requirement of 150%, providing sufficient buffer for potential future credit losses.
- **Capital Adequacy**: The new core capital adequacy ratio improved gradually from 13.26% (mid-2025) to 13.77% (end-2025), fully meeting regulatory requirements and supporting further business expansion.
- **Loan-to-Deposit Ratio (LDR)**: LDR increased from 92.26% (mid-2025) to 94.64% (end-2025), reflecting that the bank's loan expansion pace has picked up slightly in the second half of 2025.
- **Asset-Liability Ratio**: The ratio stabilized between 90.7% and 91.3%, which is a normal level for commercial banks.

## 4. Broker Research Consensus
Broker analysts hold a mixed view on Ping An Bank's 2025 performance:
- **Dongxing Securities** issued two buy ratings for the 2025 mid-year and third-quarter reports, arguing that the decline of revenue and profit has narrowed, interest spread has stabilized month-on-month, and retail asset quality has improved, so there is allocation value at current valuations.
- **Guoxin Securities** issued three neutral ratings, pointing out that the overall interest rate environment still brings pressure to net interest income, and the improvement of fundamentals is still not clear enough.
- **Consensus Valuation**: The average forecast for 2026 EPS is 2.08-2.24 CNY, corresponding to a forecast PE of 5.1-5.2x, which is an extremely low valuation level in the current A-share market, indicating that most downside risks have already been priced in.

## 6. Key Insights for Traders
1. **Marginal Improvement**: The downward trend of revenue and profit is gradually slowing, and interest spread has stabilized after continuous decline, which is a positive marginal change.
2. **Risk Resistance is Sufficient**: Stable asset quality and high provision coverage eliminate the risk of large-scale asset impairment, so the downside space of the stock price is limited at current valuations.
3. **Valuation Attractiveness**: The forecast PE of less than 6x for 2026 already fully reflects market pessimism about banking profit growth. If the macro policy supports interest rate stabilization and credit expansion, there will be obvious valuation repair opportunities.
4. **Downside Risk**: If the macroeconomic recovery is lower than expected, credit non-performing rate may rise, and net interest spread may continue to be under pressure, which will restrain profit repair.
