# Fundamental Analysis Report: Kweichow Moutai (600519.SS)
## Analysis Date: 2025-03-20

### 1. Company Profile
Kweichow Moutai (600519.SS) is China's leading premium baijiu (liquor) producer, with a centuries-old iconic brand and unrivaled pricing power in the high-end baijiu market. It is a core blue-chip holding in the A-share market, widely recognized for stable cash flow generation and strong balance sheet resilience.

### 2. Key Financial Performance (Latest Periods)
Based on the latest published financial reports up to 2025Q3:
- **Profitability**: Gross margin remained stable at 91.29% in 2025Q3 cumulative, maintaining the company's ultra-high profitability level that is unmatched among Chinese consumer companies. Cumulative return on equity (ROE) for the first three quarters of 2025 reached 24.64%, outperforming nearly all peer consumer staples companies. Earnings per share (EPS) hit 51.53 for 2025Q3 cumulative, up from 36.18 in 2025 H1, reflecting steady quarterly earnings accretion.
- **Growth Trend**: 2025Q3 cumulative total operating revenue reached RMB 130.9 billion, a 6.32% year-over-year (YoY) increase; net profit attributable to parent shareholders hit RMB 64.63 billion, a 6.25% YoY increase. This represents a moderation in growth compared to 2024 full year, which delivered 12.77% YoY revenue growth and 15.38% YoY net profit growth. The slowdown is largely driven by a higher base effect from 2024 and the company's mature market positioning as a premium leader, rather than fundamental weakness.
- **Balance Sheet Health**: Net assets per share (BPS) stood at 205.28 in 2025Q3, up 10.6% from 185.57 at the end of 2024, showing steady net asset accumulation. Debt-to-asset ratio is only 14.69%, indicating extremely low leverage and no material solvency risk. Operating cash flow per share is 30.50 in 2025Q3 cumulative, reflecting strong cash generation from operations, supported by Moutai's pre-order business model with distributors.

### 3. Institutional Broker Consensus (Recent Period)
Among the latest 5 research reports from leading Chinese brokerages (published in March 2026, within the recent analysis window):
- 4 out of 5 reports maintain a "Buy" rating, 1 gives an "Overweight" rating, indicating overwhelmingly positive institutional sentiment toward the stock.
- Consensus EPS forecasts: 2025 EPS is projected at RMB 71.07 - 72.55, implying a forward P/E of 20.0 - 20.5; 2026 EPS projected at RMB 71.26 - 77.85, forward P/E 18.6 - 20.5; 2027 EPS projected at RMB 72.78 - 84.02, forward P/E 17.3 - 20.1. Analysts broadly expect gradual moderate EPS growth over the next three years, with slight valuation contraction as growth moderates, which is viewed as reasonable for a mature consumer staple leader.

### 4. Key Insights for Traders
#### Core Strengths:
1. Unmatched brand moat and pricing power, with gross margins staying above 91% for multiple consecutive periods, demonstrating consistent profitability.
2. Extremely healthy balance sheet with minimal leverage and strong operating cash flow, making Moutai highly resilient to macroeconomic downturns.
3. Valuations at ~20x forward 2025 P/E are in line with historical average valuations for the stock, not overstretched given its quality.

#### Key Risks:
1. Growth has moderated to mid-single digits in 2025, which may disappoint short-term traders seeking high double-digit earnings upside.
2. Premium baijiu demand can be mildly sensitive to macro consumption trends, though Moutai's core high-net-worth customer base is relatively resilient.
